Beef Gyudon

Description

Thinly sliced beef and tender onions cooked in savory-sweet sauce, Gyudon, or Japanese Beef Rice Bowl, is synonymous with comfort. It has been a staple in Japanese cuisine for over 150 years! It's a perfect quick meal for a busy weeknight!

The name gyudon was coined by Eikichi Matsuda in the late 1800s. Matsuda is the owner of Japan's most famous Tokyo-based, beef bowl chain, Yoshinoya.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 scallion
  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced beef
  • 1/2 cup dashi
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoon sake
  • 2 tablespoon mirin
  • 3 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 servings steamed white rice
  • pickled red ginger

Steps

  1. First, thinly slice the onion.
  2. Next, cut the green onions diagonally into thin slices.
  3. Then, take out the meat from the freezer. Cut the sliced meat into pieces 3 inches (7.6 cm) wide.
  4. In a large frying pan (do not turn on the heat yet), add the dashi, sake, mirin, soy sauce, and sugar. Mix to combine.
  5. Next, add the onion slices and spread them throughout the pan, separating the onion layers.
  6. Then, add the meat on top of the onions. Separate the thin slices of beef so the meat covers the onions.
  7. Cover the pan with a lid and start cooking over medium heat.
  8. When the meat is cooked, skim off the scum and fat from the broth with a fine-mesh skimmer. Turn down the heat to simmer and continue cooking, covered, for 3-4 minutes.
  9. Sprinkle the green onions on top and cook covered for another minute. If you like to add beaten eggs, you can add at this step.
  10. In a large donburi bowl, add the steamed rice and drizzle the remaining sauce on top.
  11. Put the beef and onion mixture on top. If you'd like, drizzle the remaining sauce on top. Top with the pickled red ginger. Enjoy!
